## 1. THE "EBITDA" CHARADE (Quality of Earnings)
- **SBC Addict:** Look at **Stock Based Compensation** as a % of Cash Flow. If FCF is only positive because they pay staff in dilution, it's fake.
- **The Adjustment Gap:** Compare **GAAP Net Income** vs. **Adjusted EBITDA**. If the gap is widening, they are engineering the beat.

## 2. THE WORKING CAPITAL ROT (Forensic Audit)
- **Channel Stuffing Check:** Is **Accounts Receivable** growing faster than Revenue? (They are booking sales but not getting paid).
- **Inventory Bloat:** Is **Inventory** growing faster than Sales? (The product is rotting on the shelf).
- *Signal:* Rising Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) or Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO) is the "smoke" before the fire.

## 3. THE CAPITAL ALLOCATION SIN
- **Buyback Suicide:** Are they repurchasing shares at All-Time High valuations (P/E > 30)? This destroys shareholder value.
- **Insider Exit:** Are executives selling into the buyback? (Using company cash to provide their own exit liquidity).

## 4. THE MACRO TRIGGER (The Pop)
- **The Refi Wall:** Look at the Debt Maturity profile. Do they have billions due in the next 12-24 months that must be refinanced at higher rates?
- **Operating Leverage Reversal:** If Revenue drops 10%, does EPS drop 50%? (High fixed costs).

## 5. THE DEMAND SUSTAINABILITY AUDIT (Customer Economics)
- **The Gold Rush Test:** Is the company selling picks and shovels to miners who aren't finding gold? Are this company's largest customers generating positive ROI from what they buy?
- **Revenue borrowed from the future:** If customers are spending on faith (capex ahead of revenue), the demand is cyclical, not structural. Cisco's telco customers (2000), Peloton's COVID buyers (2021), and mining equipment in commodity super-cycles (2012) all looked like structural demand until the ROI failed to materialize.
- **The specific check:** What is the customer's revenue or cost savings attributable to this company's product vs. what they spend on it? If this data doesn't exist or is hand-waved, that IS the red flag.
- **Capex cycle fragility:** What % of revenue depends on customers' capital expenditure budgets (not operating budgets)? Capex is discretionary and gets cut first in downturns.

## 6. THE MOAT EROSION STRESS TEST
- **Model the decline:** If core revenue drops 20% over 5 years (moat weakening, disintermediation), what happens to margins, FCF, and the balance sheet? Does the business survive or implode?
- **The fixed cost trap:** What percentage of costs are truly fixed? A high-margin business with high fixed costs is fragile in reverse.
- **Cash runway:** At what revenue decline rate does FCF turn negative? How many years of runway does the balance sheet provide?
- **Management response:** Would management cut costs fast enough, or would they keep "investing" through the decline?
